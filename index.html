Nissan S13
The Nissan S13 SX200 was a sports coupe produced by the Japanese automaker Nissan between 1988 and 1994. It was available in both rear-wheel drive and all-wheel drive configurations, and was powered by a range of inline-four and inline-six engines. The S13 SX200 was known for its sleek and aerodynamic body style, as well as its performance and handling capabilities. It was a popular choice among enthusiasts and remains a classic and sought-after vehicle today.

Mitsubishi Evo
The Mitsubishi Lancer Evolution is a high-performance sports car that has been produced by the Japanese automaker since 1992. It is based on the Mitsubishi Lancer, but features numerous modifications to increase its power and handling capabilities. The Evolution has a 2.0 liter turbocharged engine that delivers impressive acceleration and top speeds. It also features advanced suspension and braking systems, as well as an advanced all-wheel drive system that allows it to excel in both wet and dry conditions. The Lancer Evolution has gained a reputation as a reliable and fun-to-drive car that is perfect for enthusiasts looking for a thrilling driving experience.

Toyoya AE86
The Toyota AE86 is a small, rear-wheel drive sports car that was produced from 1983 to 1987. It was known for its lightweight and nimble handling, making it a popular choice for drifting and other motorsports. The AE86 was also featured in the popular anime and manga series "Initial D," further boosting its popularity among car enthusiasts. Despite its small size, the AE86 was powered by a 4A-GE engine that produced around 112 horsepower, making it a quick and agile car on the road. Despite its discontinuation in the 1980s, the Toyota AE86 remains a beloved classic among car enthusiasts today.
